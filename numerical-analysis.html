Numerical Analysis

Module Overview

This module introduced me to the foundations of numerical and statistical analysis used in Artificial Intelligence and data-driven decision-making. It helped me understand how data can be explored, summarised, visualised, interpreted, and used to support meaningful conclusions.

Learning Outcomes

  • Develop an understanding of descriptive and inferential statistics.
  • Apply statistical analysis techniques using R programming.
  • Interpret statistical outputs such as p-values, confidence intervals, regression results, and visual summaries.
  • Use plots, summary tables, and statistical tests to understand patterns in data.
  • Reflect on the role of statistical thinking in Artificial Intelligence and machine learning.

Artefacts

  • R programming practice activities
  • Summary tables and descriptive statistics
  • Histograms, boxplots, and bar charts
  • Hypothesis testing activities
  • Linear regression and logistic regression outputs
  • Individual reflective work on statistical learning

Skills Developed

  • Basic R programming confidence
  • Data interpretation and analysis
  • Understanding of statistical evidence
  • Numeracy and critical thinking
  • Ability to connect statistics with AI and machine learning concepts

Reflection

As a beginner in statistical analysis and R programming, this module helped me move from simply looking at data to understanding how evidence can be extracted from data. I learned that statistical analysis is not only about running code, but also about asking the right question, choosing the right method, and interpreting the result carefully.

I found some concepts challenging at first, especially p-values, confidence intervals, regression outputs, and deciding which statistical test to use. However, by practising with real datasets and visual outputs, I gradually developed better confidence in interpreting results and explaining them in simple language.

Going forward, I aim to strengthen my practical R skills and apply statistical thinking more confidently in machine learning, predictive modelling, and AI-related problem-solving.
