Knowledge Representation & Reasoning

Module Overview

This module introduced the foundations of Knowledge Representation and Reasoning (KRR) in Artificial Intelligence. It explored how knowledge can be structured, represented, and processed so that intelligent systems can make decisions, reason logically, and solve problems effectively.

Learning Outcomes

  • Critique the need for formal approaches to knowledge representation and reasoning.
  • Review critically the properties of knowledge-based systems.
  • Evaluate different modelling techniques used in Artificial Intelligence.
  • Examine and apply reasoning approaches for solving knowledge-based problems.

Artefacts

  • Collaborative discussion summaries
  • Research notes and literature reviews
  • Reflective learning activities
  • Knowledge modelling exercises
  • Problem-solving and reasoning examples

Skills Developed

  • Critical thinking and analytical reasoning
  • Understanding of AI knowledge structures
  • Research and literature evaluation
  • Problem-solving using logical approaches
  • Academic writing and reflective practice

Reflection

This module helped me understand that Artificial Intelligence is not only about machine learning algorithms, but also about how knowledge is organised and processed. I learned that intelligent systems require structured representations of knowledge to reason effectively and support decision-making.

Initially, concepts such as ontologies, semantic networks, rule-based systems, and logical reasoning appeared abstract and difficult to understand. However, through discussions, reading academic literature, and applying examples, I gradually developed a better understanding of how these concepts are connected to real-world intelligent systems.

One important learning point for me was understanding the importance of representing knowledge correctly so that systems can interpret information consistently and make logical decisions. This module also strengthened my research, critical evaluation, and reflective learning abilities.

Going forward, I would like to further explore how Knowledge Representation and Reasoning can support AI-driven automation, intelligent assistants, and enterprise decision-making systems.
